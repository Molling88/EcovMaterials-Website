The Problem We Address

Electronic waste is one of the fastest‑growing waste streams globally—yet it also contains valuable critical minerals essential to clean energy, advanced manufacturing, and digital infrastructure.

At the same time, supply chains for these materials are increasingly vulnerable, opaque, and environmentally costly. Valuable resources are lost, environmental risks increase, and recovery systems remain fragmented.

EcoV exists to close this gap.

What EcoV Does

EcoV operates at the coordination layer of the circular economy, collecting, securing, and preparing end‑of‑life electronics and industrial components into certified, traceable critical minerals feedstock.

Rather than relying on capital‑intensive processing infrastructure, we focus on structuring fragmented waste flows, ensuring quality and traceability, and supplying domestic and regional refiners with reliable, compliant feedstock.

This asset‑light approach enables scalable impact while maintaining high environmental and social standards.

Why It Matters

EcoV’s model responds to the growing demand for critical minerals, the rise of circular‑economy policies, and increasing expectations around ESG governance and supply‑chain transparency.

By formalizing recovery pathways, we help reduce environmental harm, strengthen material security, and enable responsible sourcing—without shifting risks downstream.

Impact by Design

Environmental and social impact are integral to EcoV’s approach.

Our work reduces environmental and health risks associated with unstructured e‑waste handling, improves traceability and accountability across recovery systems, and supports safer, more inclusive economic participation.

We believe resilient supply chains and inclusive outcomes are mutually reinforcing.

Who We Work With

EcoV collaborates with electronics producers, municipalities, recyclers, refiners, research institutions, and impact partners.

Our role is to connect actors across the value chain—providing structure, coordination, and trust where it is most needed.
